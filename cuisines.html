Traditional Dutch Cuisines

Stroopwafel

Stroopwafel is a beloved Dutch treat made of two thin waffle layers with a caramel syrup filling in the middle. It’s a delightful combination of sweet and chewy textures, perfect with a hot drink like coffee or tea.

Poffertjes

Poffertjes are tiny, fluffy pancakes typically served with butter and powdered sugar. These mini delights are popular at festivals and markets, often enjoyed as a sweet snack during the colder months.

Erwtensoep

Erwtensoep, or Dutch pea soup, is a thick, hearty dish made from split peas, pork, and vegetables. It’s a traditional winter comfort food, often served with rye bread and sausage, making it a fulfilling meal.

Bitterballen

Bitterballen are deep-fried meatballs, filled with a rich ragout, often served as a snack with mustard at Dutch pubs. They’re crunchy on the outside, creamy on the inside, and perfect for sharing with friends.

Haring

Haring, or raw herring fish, is often served with onions and pickles, and eaten by holding the fish by the tail. It's a quintessential Dutch street food, typically enjoyed as a quick snack.

Kroket

Kroket is a deep-fried, breadcrumb-coated roll filled with beef or veal ragout. It’s often eaten as a quick snack on the go or served as part of a meal, especially in sandwiches.

Boerenkoolstamppot

Boerenkoolstamppot is a traditional Dutch dish made from mashed potatoes mixed with kale, often served with smoked sausage or bacon. It's a hearty and comforting winter meal.

Patat

Patat, or Dutch fries, are thicker than regular French fries and are often served with a variety of toppings, such as mayonnaise, peanut sauce, or satay. They're a beloved street food in the Netherlands.

Kibbeling

Kibbeling is battered and deep-fried fish, typically cod, served with garlic sauce. It's a popular snack at Dutch fish markets and a must-try for seafood lovers.

Hutspot

Hutspot is a Dutch dish made from mashed potatoes, carrots, and onions. It's traditionally served with a sausage and is a popular winter meal in many Dutch households.

Sate

Sate is Indonesian-inspired skewered grilled meat served with a rich peanut sauce. It’s popular in the Netherlands, often served as a snack or appetizer.

Rijsttafel

Rijsttafel, or "rice table," is an Indonesian-inspired Dutch dish that consists of a variety of small dishes, often served with rice. It’s a flavorful and diverse meal, perfect for sharing with friends.

Bami Goreng

Bami Goreng is a Dutch-Indonesian fried noodle dish with vegetables, chicken, or shrimp. It’s a quick and flavorful meal that can be found in many Dutch households and restaurants.

Indonesian Satay

Indonesian Satay is a popular dish in the Netherlands, consisting of skewered and grilled meat served with a peanut sauce. It's commonly found in Dutch-Indonesian restaurants, a testament to the country's colonial past.
