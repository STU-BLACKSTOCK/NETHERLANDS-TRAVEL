DELFT

Places to Visit!

Here are some placed you can visit in Delft

The Stadhuis Delft

The Stadhuis (City Hall) in Delft is a beautiful blend of Renaissance and Gothic architecture, located in the heart of the city’s historic market square. With its elegant façade, intricate detailing, and grand clock tower, the building stands as a testament to Delft’s rich civic heritage. Inside, visitors can admire the opulent council chamber and learn about the city's past through fascinating exhibits. The Stadhuis is not only a working government building but also a cultural landmark, making it a must-visit for anyone exploring Delft’s charming city center.

The Vermeer Centrum

The Vermeer Centrum in Delft is a captivating museum dedicated to the life and works of the renowned Dutch painter, Johannes Vermeer. Located in the artist's hometown, the center offers a deep dive into Vermeer’s legacy, featuring detailed exhibitions on his masterpieces, techniques, and the 17th-century world he depicted. Visitors can explore interactive displays, view replicas of his famous paintings, and learn about the artist’s creative process. The Vermeer Centrum provides an inspiring and educational experience for art lovers, making it a must-see for those fascinated by this iconic Dutch artist.

The Prinsenhof Museum

The Prinsenhof Museum in Delft is a captivating journey through Dutch history and royal heritage. Housed in a former palace, it showcases the life of William of Orange, the founder of the Dutch nation, with exhibits detailing his role in the Dutch Revolt and the formation of the Netherlands. Visitors can explore beautifully restored rooms, admire historical artifacts, and learn about the turbulent yet transformative period of the 16th century. The museum also offers stunning views of Delft’s picturesque canals, making it a must-visit destination for history and culture enthusiasts.
