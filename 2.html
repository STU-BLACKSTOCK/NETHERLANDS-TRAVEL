ROTTERDAM

Places to Visit!

Here are some placed you can visit in Rotterdam

Markthal

The Markthal in Rotterdam is a culinary and architectural marvel, offering a vibrant experience for food lovers and sightseers alike. This impressive horseshoe-shaped market hall houses over 100 food stalls, serving everything from fresh local produce to international delicacies. With its colorful ceiling mural, known as the “Horn of Plenty,” and an inviting blend of restaurants and specialty shops, the Markthal is both a feast for the eyes and the taste buds. Discover the best of Dutch flavors and global treats in this lively marketplace at the heart of Rotterdam.

Rotterdam Central Station

Rotterdam Central Station is an architectural icon and a bustling gateway to the city. Known for its striking design featuring a dynamic, angled roof and expansive glass façade, the station reflects Rotterdam’s modern, innovative spirit. Inside, travelers can explore a range of shops, cafes, and public art, all while enjoying seamless connections to destinations across the Netherlands and Europe. Convenient and stylish, Rotterdam Central Station is more than just a transit hub—it’s a vibrant landmark at the heart of the city.

Erasmus Bridge

The Erasmus Bridge, or “The Swan” as locals call it, is a symbol of Rotterdam’s modern skyline and architectural innovation. Stretching across the River Maas, this elegant, asymmetrical bridge connects the city’s northern and southern districts. Known for its sleek design and graceful pylon, it offers stunning views of the waterfront and the surrounding cityscape. Whether crossing by foot, bike, or tram, a visit to the Erasmus Bridge is a must for panoramic photo ops and an unforgettable view of Rotterdam's urban charm.
