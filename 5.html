HAARLEM

Places to Visit!

Here are some placed you can visit in Haarlem

Corrie ten Boom House

The Corrie ten Boom House in Haarlem is a poignant site dedicated to the memory of the Dutch resistance during World War II. This historic house was once the home of Corrie ten Boom and her family, who bravely sheltered Jews from the Nazis. Visitors can tour the rooms where the family hid people and learn about their courageous acts of compassion. The museum offers a powerful, moving experience that highlights the importance of resilience, faith, and standing up for what’s right. It’s a must-see for those interested in stories of heroism during dark times.

Molen de Adriaan Museum

Molen de Adriaan in Haarlem is a charming, historic windmill that offers a fascinating glimpse into Dutch milling traditions. Located along the Spaarne River, this fully restored 18th-century windmill now serves as a museum, showcasing the history of milling, local heritage, and the windmill’s important role in Haarlem’s development. Visitors can climb to the top for stunning views of the city, explore the inner workings of the mill, and learn about the craft of milling. Molen de Adriaan is a captivating stop for those interested in Dutch culture and engineering history.

The Sint-Bavokerk (Church of St. Bavo)

The Sint-Bavokerk (Church of St. Bavo) in Haarlem is a stunning example of Gothic architecture and a must-see landmark in the city. Famous for its grand interior, the church houses an impressive organ, once played by Mozart, and beautiful stained glass windows. Visitors can admire the intricate details of its architecture, explore the historic tombs, and enjoy the serene atmosphere inside. Located in the heart of Haarlem, the Sint-Bavokerk is not only a place of worship but also a captivating destination for history and art lovers alike.
